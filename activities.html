Daily Activities & Recap

Follow our 7-day journey through workshops, discussions, and creative sessions.

Day 1: [Title]

The first day was the moment we truly got to know each other during the workshops, the first one being dedicated to this very purpose. Each of us received a watch where we scheduled a different meeting every hour. We talked about various topics, discovered shared passions, and built connections that helped us in the next activities.

The Human Bingo activity aimed at discovering each other’s talents, passions, or just random facts about one another, as we worked on completing our bingo cards.

After getting to know each other on a deeper level, we were divided into teams and moved on to the next activity—a treasure hunt around the city that hosted us, Bansko. It was an incredibly fun experience, filled with laughter and creative challenges, including making videos that will always remind us of these special moments.

Returning to our accommodation as the day was coming to an end, we spent the evening together playing board games and enjoying each other’s company.

Day 2: [Title]

Our second day in Bansko started on a truly heartwarming note as we created both a gift box and a Good Vibes Box, writing kind and uplifting messages to each other, which helped us build a more sincere and positive group atmosphere.

To boost our energy, we played a fun warm-up game called Fruit Salad, which brought lots of laughter and set a lively tone for the day. We then moved on to our main workshop sessions, where we were divided into groups of six and created various outputs such as posters, presentations, videos, and even short theatre performances based on given topics, showcasing our creativity in different ways.

Later, we worked in smaller groups focusing on solutions to rural area problems, using the Coffee Hub system, which made the workshop more dynamic and interactive by allowing us to rotate between groups, exchange ideas, and gain new perspectives. At the end of the sessions, we gathered to share the most discussed and impactful ideas of the day, reflecting on our collective work.

We concluded the day with a vibrant Turkish Culture Night, where we served Turkish coffee, danced Zeybek, played Kahoot, and had fun dancing to Erik Dalı, making it a perfect ending to a day full of creativity, collaboration, and positive energy and We learned about Romanian culture, we tried traditional foods, continued with a fun Kahoot game, and ended the night with dances.

Day 3: [Title]

We kicked off the day with a fun energizer game where we held each other’s fingers and tested our reflexes. After that, we joined Ana’s workshop, which focused on finding creative solutions to everyday problems. Each team presented their ideas in unique ways—some made short movies, others performed sketches or created advertisements.

Following a short break, we continued with the afternoon workshop, where we learned more about real organizations in our countries that help people and support communities.

Later, we had some time to relax before ending the day with a Serbian intercultural night. We discovered many interesting things about Serbian traditions and culture.

Day 4: [Title]

During our 4th day, Turkey presented their fun workshop that made us guess issues in our society without using certain words. After this fun icebreaker, we had to create a project about these problems and their widespread impact.

Moreover, we had to elaborate on our opinions in a heated debate, during which we learned a lot about entrepreneurship. Moving on to another workshop, the wonderful Serbian Ana spoke about cultural acceptance and changing mindsets.

As the day was coming to an end, we indulged in Italian and Bulgarian culture through their traditional dances, foods, and interesting presentations.

Day 5: [Title]

We started the day with a stretching and warm-up activity, which helped us feel more energetic and ready. Then, we prepared and shared information about youth entrepreneurs from our countries, including short biographies. We talked about their motivations and what they have achieved, which was very inspiring.

During the AI workshop, we discussed technology and its role in our project. We also did an activity where we tried to distinguish between real photos and AI-generated images. After that, we started working on our final project, making plans and organizing tasks to develop our ideas. Finally, we ended the day with a relaxing spa activity.

Day 6: [Title]

One of the best days of this exchange has to be Saturday and the youth excursion. From early in the morning, we started our trip to one of the biggest monasteries here in Bulgaria, the Rila Monastery. We spent the morning there getting to know more about its Orthodox culture, and after the visit, our trip continued to Blagoevgrad, a city on our way back to Bansko.

Altogether, we spent 3 hours in the city exploring it. We chose to go to the zoo and later just chill around the city. After our trip, we all spent time in the lobby and made our “blanket cult” by putting blankets over our heads and jumping together as one happy family.

Day 7: [Title]

Day 7 began with us putting the final touches on our project outcomes, making sure everything we had worked on throughout the week was complete and well-prepared. It was a moment of reflection, where we could clearly see how much effort, creativity, and teamwork had gone into our results.

After that, we gathered for the final evaluation, sharing our experiences, thoughts, and personal growth during the project. It was inspiring to hear different perspectives and realize how meaningful this journey had been for all of us.

The day concluded with the Youthpass certificate ceremony, a special and emotional moment where we received recognition for our learning, participation, and achievements. It marked not only the end of the project, but also the friendships, memories, and skills that we will carry with us long after this experience.
