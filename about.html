Aims and Objectives

The primary aim of this Erasmus+ project was to empower young people through non-formal education, intercultural exchange, and active participation. The project sought to create an inclusive environment where participants could develop both personally and professionally while engaging with peers from diverse backgrounds.

The key objectives of the project included:

  • To promote entrepreneurship and innovative thinking among young people
  • To encourage active citizenship and participation in community life
  • To enhance intercultural understanding, tolerance, and mutual respect
  • To develop essential soft skills such as communication, teamwork, and problem-solving
  • To provide participants with practical knowledge and tools applicable in their future education and careers
  • To strengthen cooperation and networking between participants from different countries

Through achieving these objectives, the project aimed to contribute to the overall development of confident, responsible, and socially engaged young individuals.

The Erasmus+ Programme

  • Youth Exchanges → short trips (5–21 days) with teens from other countries; fun + learning, almost fully funded.
  • University Exchange → study abroad for a semester/year through Erasmus+ Programme.
  • Internships (Traineeships) → work experience abroad with financial support.
  • Youth Participation Projects → debates, activism, working on social issues.
  • Solidarity Projects → you create your own local project and get funding.
  • Volunteering (ESC) → volunteer abroad with all costs covered via European Solidarity Corps.
  • Support / Inclusion Projects → programs helping teens with confidence, mental health, or fewer opportunities.

The "Snowball Effect"

We believe it's import to have a one good idea, put trust in it and go to take action and promote it everywhere possible. Starting with a small group of people and later expanding it by different kinds of actions or simply telling it to your friends can be a big change for our whole society. One little thing can cause a big impact on our generation.
